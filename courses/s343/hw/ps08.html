∗2 Find a dataset of interest to you, placing focus on a single quantitative variable.
(a) produce a histogram of your data.
(b) Among the continuous distributions we have studied, choose one which seems (to you) the best model for your data. Overlay a plot of this distribution on your histogram from part (a).
(c) Experimenting with various base kernels (rectagular, triangular, etc.), find a pdf using kernel density estimation that appears to fit your data well. Plot the histogram of part (a) again, and overlay a plot of your estimated pdf much as in part (b).
(d) Repeat the process of determining a pdf through kernel density estimation. This time, however, use only half of your data (call this the "training data") to obtain your approximate pdf. Then plot a histogram of the unused data and overlay a plot of your pdf. How does it do?
