\ni
Types we handle and relevant trig. identities:
\bq
{\small
$$ \begin{array}{ccc}
  \mbox{{\ul{integral type}}}
	& \phantom{XXX} & \mbox{{\ul{trig. identity}}} \\[10pt]
  \int \sin^m x \cos^n x\,dx, \quad m\;\mbox{or}\; n\; \mbox{odd}
	& \phantom{XXX} & \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2\theta = 1 \\[8pt]
  \int \sin^m x \cos^n x\,dx, \quad m, n\; \mbox{even}
	& & \sin^2\theta = \frac{1}{2}[1 - \cos(2\theta)] \;\mbox{and}\;
	\cos^2\theta = \frac{1}{2}[1 + \cos(2\theta)] \\[8pt]
  \int \sqrt{1 + \cos(mx)}\,dx & & 
	2 \cos^2\theta = 1 + \cos(2\theta) \\[8pt]
  \int \tan^m x \sec^n x \, dx, \quad m, n > 1
	& & 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \\[8pt]
  \int \cot^m x \csc^n x \, dx, \quad m, n > 1
	& & 1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta \\[8pt]
  \int \sin(mx) \sin(nx) \, dx, \quad m \ne n
	& & \sin(m\theta)\sin(n\theta) = \frac{1}{2}[\cos((m-n)\theta)
	- \cos((m+n)\theta)] \\[8pt]
  \int \sin(mx) \cos(nx) \, dx, \quad m \ne n
	& & \sin(m\theta)\cos(n\theta) = \frac{1}{2}[\sin((m-n)\theta)
	+ \sin((m+n)\theta)] \\[8pt]
  \int \cos(mx) \cos(nx) \, dx, \quad m \ne n
	& & \cos(m\theta)\cos(n\theta) = \frac{1}{2}[\cos((m-n)\theta)
	+ \cos((m+n)\theta)] \\[8pt]
  \end{array} $$
}
\eq

\ni
But, frequently the above identities only serve to reduce things to
a special case you must be able to handle some other way.  Some of
these special cases:
\bi
\item[] $\ds{\int \tan x\,dx},\;$ handled via substitution
\item[] $\ds{\int \sec^3 x\, dx},\;$ handled by parts (see p.~459)
\item[] $\ds{\int \tan^m x \sec^2 x \,dx},\;$ handled via substitution
\item[]
  $\ds{\int \sec x\,dx},\;$ handled via substitution, after
  multiplying through by $\ds{\frac{\sec x + \tan x}{\sec x + \tan x}}$
\item[] $\ds{\int \tan x \sec^n x \,dx},\;$ handled via substitution
\ei
