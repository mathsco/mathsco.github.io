\vs{0.2in}
\ni
Integration by parts formula:
$$ \int u\, dv \= uv - \int v\,du $$

\ni
Remarks
\bi
\item
  Counterpart to product rule for differentiation
\item
  Applicable for definite integrals as well:
  $$ \int_a^b u \,dv \= uv\Big|_a^b - \int_a^b v\,du. $$
\item
  Technique used when integrands have the form:
  \bi
  \item
	$p(x) e^{ax}$, $\;p(x) e^{ax},\;$ where $p(x)$ is a
	polynomial, $a$ a constant \\
	Let $u$ be the polynomial part; number of iterations equals
	degree of $p$
  \item
	$p(x) \cos(bx)$, $\;p(x) \sin(bx),\;$ where $p(x)$ is a
	polynomial, $b$ a constant \\
	Let $u$ be the polynomial part; number of iterations equals
	degree of $p$
  \item
	$e^{ax} \cos(bx)$, $\;e^{ax} \sin(bx),\;$ where $a$, $b$ are constants \\
	No rule for which part $u$ equals; requires 2 iterations and algebra
  \ei
\ei

\ni
Some integrals (less obviously) done by parts:
\bi
\item $\ds{\int \ln x \,dx}\;\;$ (see p.~450)
\item $\ds{\int \arccos x\, dx}\;\;$ (and other inverse fns; see p.~454)
\item $\ds{\int \sec^3 x \, dx}\;\;$ (see p.~459)
\ei
